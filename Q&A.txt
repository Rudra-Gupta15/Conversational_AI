What is a Savings Account?
An account designed for individuals to park their money while earning a small amount of interest.

What is a Current Account?
An account meant for businesses with no limit on the number of daily transactions; it usually offers no interest.

What is a Fixed Deposit (FD)?
An investment where you lock a sum of money for a specific period at a higher interest rate than a savings account.

What does KYC stand for?
Know Your Customer. It’s a process to verify the identity and address of customers.

What is a Check/Cheque?
A written document that orders a bank to pay a specific amount of money from a person's account to another person.

What is a Bounced Cheque?
A cheque that the bank refuses to pay because there isn't enough money in the account.

What is a Demand Draft (DD)?
A pre-paid instrument issued by a bank to make a payment to a third party; it is more secure than a cheque.

What is an Overdraft?
A facility that allows an account holder to withdraw more money than they actually have in their account, up to a certain limit.

What is a Joint Account?
An account opened in the name of two or more individuals.

What is a Nominee?
A person designated by the account holder to receive the funds in the account in the event of the holder's death.

Section 2: Cards & Digital Banking
What is a Debit Card?
A card linked directly to your bank account; money is deducted instantly when you spend.

What is a Credit Card?
A card that allows you to borrow money from the bank up to a limit to make purchases, which you pay back later.

What is a PIN?
Personal Identification Number; a secret code used to authorize card transactions.

What is an ATM?
Automated Teller Machine; a machine that lets you withdraw cash and check balances without a human teller.

What is Mobile Banking?
Using a smartphone app provided by the bank to perform financial transactions.

What is Net Banking?
Accessing banking services through the bank's official website using a browser.

What is an OTP?
One-Time Password; a temporary code sent to your phone for secure online transactions.

What is NEFT?
National Electronic Funds Transfer; a way to send money between banks in batches.

What is RTGS?
Real-Time Gross Settlement; used for high-value, instant money transfers.

What is IMPS?
Immediate Payment Service; an instant 24/7 interbank electronic fund transfer service.

Section 3: Loans & Interest
What is a Loan?
Money borrowed from a bank that must be paid back with interest over a period of time.

What is a Mortgage?
A loan specifically used to purchase a home or real estate, where the property serves as collateral.

What is Collateral?
An asset (like a house or gold) that a borrower offers to a lender to secure a loan.

What is an EMI?
Equated Monthly Installment; the fixed amount paid by a borrower to a lender every month.

What is the Repo Rate?
The rate at which the central bank (like RBI) lends money to commercial banks.

What is a Credit Score?
A number that represents a person's creditworthiness based on their borrowing history.

What is Simple Interest?
Interest calculated only on the principal amount borrowed or deposited.

What is Compound Interest?
Interest calculated on the principal plus the interest that has already accumulated.

What is a Personal Loan?
An unsecured loan used for various personal expenses like travel or weddings.

What is a Default?
The failure to repay a loan or meet the legal obligations of a loan agreement.

Section 4: Bank Operations & Security
What is a Bank Statement?
A monthly record of all transactions (deposits and withdrawals) in an account.

What is a Passbook?
A small book issued to savings account holders to record all transactions manually or via machine.

What is a Ledger Balance?
The total amount of money in an account at the start of the business day.

What is an Available Balance?
The amount of money in your account that you can actually spend right now.

What is Phishing?
A fraudulent attempt to steal personal information like passwords by pretending to be a bank via email or SMS.

What is a Dormant Account?
An account that has seen no customer-initiated transactions for a long period (usually 2 years).

What is a Bank Vault?
A secure space where money, valuables, and records are stored.

What is a Locker Facility?
A safe deposit box rented by a customer to store jewelry or important documents.

What is a Bank Teller?
An employee at the bank counter who handles cash deposits, withdrawals, and other basic tasks.

What is a Branch Manager?
The person in charge of the operations and employees of a specific bank location.

Section 5: General Finance Terms
What is Inflation?
The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising, reducing purchasing power.

What is a Central Bank?
The main institution that manages a country's currency and monetary policy (e.g., RBI in India, Federal Reserve in the US).

What is Liquidity?
The ease with which an asset can be converted into cash without affecting its market price.

What is a Bad Debt?
A loan that is unlikely to be repaid by the borrower.

What is an NPA?
Non-Performing Asset; a loan where the interest or principal has been overdue for a specific period (usually 90 days).

What is Direct Deposit?
An electronic transfer of a payment (like a salary) directly from the payer's account to the recipient's account.

What is a Withdrawal Slip?
A form used in a bank branch to take money out of an account.

What is a Deposit Slip?
A form used to put cash or cheques into a bank account.

What is the IFSC Code?
Indian Financial System Code; a unique alphanumeric code used to identify a specific bank branch for electronic transfers.

What is a MICR Code?
Magnetic Ink Character Recognition; a code used mainly for the processing and clearance of cheques.
